Life Tabernacle Apostolic Church of Porterville has an amazing heritage!  We are so thankful and appreciative for every person who sacrificed so much for both this city and church.  Your hard work and prayers has not gone unnoticed or forgotten!

 

"Upon this rock I will build my church, and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it."  (Matthew 16:8)

 

*******

 

In Loving Memory of Sis. Vivian Webb - July 14, 2013

 

*******

 

"It was in 1958 that Chois and Vivian Webb moved to Porterville to build a church.  With Sis. Webb's retirement from teaching school, they bought the property at 939 North Main Street with a dream and a vision.


At the time of purchase, there was no electricity or water on the property.  Pastor Webb used to haul fifty gallon barrels of water to help water some trees, and to help loosen the bed pan clay so they could dig the foundation.  The property was in a gully, so Pastor Webb thought he'd build a basement for Sunday School rooms and for fellowship, then fill in dirt around it for a parking lot.  There was still a lot of digging in the hard pan red clay.  Pastor Webb and son, Bo Webb, painstakingly dug that foundation by hand.  The church was built by brick, buying thirty or more bricks at a time and buying steel to reinforce the structure as concrete was poured inside the bricks.  This church was built by hand, and paid for as they went along.  A septic tank was put in and water from the county was acquired from behind the  church and under the railroad tracks.  There was a saint in the church that helped Pastor Webb and Bo build along the way, Bro. Joel Gentles.  Bro. I. H. Terry, Bro. Jimmy Davis, and Bro. Paul Wilson sent men, bricks, and steel to help them many weekends.  It was a long, arduous struggle,  but the building was finished around June of 1963.


While the building program slowly progressed, Pastor Webb held services at Murry Park on East Putnam, a Baptist church on B Street, a little church on Olive and A Street, the Legion Hall (there by the church), and in a rummage building there on the back of the property.  That building was torn down as the property was filled with "free dirt", concrete and debris that builders unloaded.  Later, all that made a big problem for Pastor Joel Michael Webb when it was his turn to build the new sanctuary on the north end of the property.


When the building was finished in 1963, Pastor Webb was down to almost no people.  The long struggle and various congregations that came and left had taken its toll.  Full of faith and hope, Pastor Webb asked Bro. & Sis. Joseph Domingues to preach a revival.  The revival sign, prayer, and fasting drew people to church.  A total of thirteen received the Holy Ghost in that revival.  The church grew, and God blessed the work.  Workers, evangelists, pastors, and a missionary went out to further the gospel.


When Pastor Webb was well into his 70's, he turned the church over to his son, who had been there since the age of four.  As Pastor Joel Michael Webb began pastoring Life Tabernacle, Apostolic Church of Porterville around 1987, again revival broke out.  Many people have been baptized and filled with the Holy Ghost, many still working for God in various places.


As the brick building filled, Pastor Webb began the plans for the vision of a new sanctuary to be built on the north end of the property.  Again, there was a huge struggle to build, but alas the new sanctuary and new parking lot were finished in 2004.  Oh, the excitement as we walked and sang unto the Lord from the old sanctuary to the new one!  Shortly after finishing the building project, Pastor Webb felt led to leave this pastorate, and Pastor Jonathan Kendrick pastored the church for exactly six years.  Since then, Bro. Steve Marchbanks has become pastor, and there stands a ripened field ready to be harvested."

 

- written by Verlaine Walden,

daughter of Founding Pastor C. B. Webb
